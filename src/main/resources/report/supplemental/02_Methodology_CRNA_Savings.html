CRNA Savings Methodology

This document provides a high-level picture and detailed validation of the calculation methodology used by the smAIrtRx OR Optimizer to measure CRNA staffing efficiency. This approach ensures a precise, productivity-adjusted measure of professional clinical requirements.

Hard Savings Recovery

CRNA savings are categorized as Direct Labor Recovery (Hard Savings) because they represent immediate opportunities to reduce payroll or contract labor expenses. This is achieved by aligning the regional labor pool supply with actual clinical demand.

Unlike physical room stacking, CRNA demand calculation excludes room preparation and cleanup padding, ensuring a precise measure of professional clinical requirements.

Calculation Logic

1. Scheduled CRNA (Supply)

This metric represents the total raw clinical capacity provided by the regional pool as recorded in the staffing schedule.

SUM(Scheduled Hours) = Scheduled CRNA (Supply)
2. Productivity Factor (Effective Supply)

The system applies a Productivity Factor (default: 85%) to the raw supply. This adjustment accounts for non-clinical time including mandatory breaks, documentation, administrative tasks, and facility transitions. This represents the "Effective Supply" available for direct patient care.

Scheduled Supply × Productivity Factor = Effective Supply
3. Clinical Demand

Demand is calculated based on raw anesthesia minutes for every case, measuring actual professional workload rather than simple physical room occupancy.

SUM(Anesthesia_End - Anesthesia_Start) / 60 = Demand Hours
4. CRNA Hours Saved

The net efficiency gain is the surplus of Effective Supply over actual clinical demand.

Effective Supply - Demand = CRNA Hours Saved
5. Monetary Value: CRNA Hours Saved ($)

The financial impact is calculated by multiplying the identified surplus hours by the modeled CRNA hourly rate ($175/hr).

CRNA Hours Saved × $175 = CRNA Hours Saved ($)

Data Integrity & Date Intersection

  • Temporal Accuracy (Date Intersection): To prevent misleading data, the system strictly analyzes staffing for dates present in both the OR case data and the CRNA schedule.
    • Mismatched Ranges: If a CRNA staffing file covers a full year but the surgical case file only covers 6 months, the Scheduled CRNA (Supply) will reflect only the hours within that 6-month intersection.
    • Automatic Filtering: Staffing hours for dates without corresponding surgical demand are automatically excluded from all totals.
    • Missing Data Handling: Dates with OR cases but no CRNA staffing data are also excluded from efficiency totals to maintain the accuracy of utilization metrics.
  • Independence: CRNA overstaffing is calculated based on actual surgical volume and is independent of physical OR room optimization. Even if rooms are consolidated, total clinical demand remains unchanged.
  • Precision: Unlike physical room stacking, the CRNA demand model excludes room preparation and cleanup padding, ensuring a pure measure of clinical work.

Mathematical Proof Sample

Example calculation for a regional pool (Columbia Region, 2025-08-25):

  • Scheduled Hours (Raw): 292.0
  • Productivity Factor: 75% (default)
  • Effective Supply: 219.00 (292.0 * 0.75)
  • Clinical Demand: 96.85
  • Result: 122.15 CRNA Hours Saved (219.00 - 96.85)
Proportional Distribution (Daily-Basis)

Regional savings are distributed to individual sites and site-days proportionally based on their share of the total regional anesthesia demand for that specific day. This ensures that the sum of all site savings exactly matches the regional total while accurately reflecting each facility's daily workload.
